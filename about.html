About the founder

Built from a good idea and a real problem.

Illuminate started when Ethan saw a booth full of beautiful 3D-printed lamps that were priced far above what many people would want to pay. That moment turned into a business idea: make stylish lamps that stay affordable and easy to buy.

Why people should care

The business solves a simple issue: many rooms have dark corners that need light, but customers do not want bulky, expensive, or generic lamps. Illuminate gives them a minimal alternative that still feels warm and personal.

Affordable Local Eco-friendly

Founder story

On a warm Sunday in late 2025, Ethan and his father found a local art trade show while browsing a newspaper. Inside the event, they discovered a booth with a 3D printer and a table of lamps, vases, and small printed objects. The lamps were beautiful, but the price felt too high for something that could be made more affordably.

That moment turned into the idea for Illuminate: create a collection of 3D-printed lamps that are bright, simple, and much more accessible for students, families, and anyone who wants a clean-looking light source.

What inspired the business

The inspiration came from seeing how much personality a small lamp can add to a room. The business is focused on making that effect available at a lower price while keeping the product design polished and minimal.

Problem: dark spaces Solution: compact light Style: minimal and modern

Design mindset

Every lamp should be useful first and decorative second. The shape should be simple enough to fit into a room without feeling loud.

Production mindset

Materials are sourced cheaply, the lamps are made in-house, and each unit is tested before it is packaged for sale.

Customer mindset

Illuminate is aimed at people who want clean-looking décor, cozy light, and a product they can see working immediately at the booth.
